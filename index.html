Abstract

As AI agents increasingly participate in mission-critical workflows‚Äîfrom finance and defense to healthcare and climate response‚Äîthe need for secure, semantically-aware, and multilingual communication becomes paramount. Existing agent systems provide orchestration and tool-calling capabilities but lack cryptographic safeguards, multilingual protocol integrity, and verifiable messaging, exposing vulnerabilities such as prompt injections, schema mimicry, cross-context spoofing, and multilingual ambiguities.

We propose ‡§∂‡•ç‡§∞‡•Åvaan, the first cryptographically grounded, multilingual model-context protocol (MCP) for agent-to-agent communication. Unlike static ciphers like AES, RSA, or TLS, ‡§∂‡•ç‡§∞‡•Åvaan integrates encryption directly into the evolving semantic layer, treating it as a learned, latent transformation conditioned on agent roles, policies, epochs, and multilingual contexts. Key innovations include a Hierarchical Keyed Protocol (HKP) for role- and context-bound encryption, a Proof-of-Protocol (PoP) for verifiable and tamper-evident messaging, and a Cipher Calibration Phase where coordinated agents continuously refine encryption based on contextual risk.

By enabling zero-reveal communication, multilingual ciphering, and tamper-evident semantics, ‡§∂‡•ç‡§∞‡•Åvaan establishes a foundational leap in secure, role-aware AI agent communication. Its effectiveness will be benchmarked using metrics such as leakage entropy, mimic risk, and decode exclusivity score, ensuring robust and context-sensitive evaluation across multilingual and semantically diverse scenarios.

SoTA Review

While frameworks like OpenAI Function Calling, LangChain Agents, and Microsoft AutoGen have accelerated task orchestration, they remain fundamentally unencrypted and semantically shallow. Transport-layer protocols like gRPC offer channel-level security but are blind to meaning‚Äîthey cannot verify agent identities, preserve intentions, or enforce roles based on the communication's content.

This model is brittle, as a single key leak compromises the entire cipher; blind to insider threats and compromises; and frozen during a breach, as it cannot adapt without a full system reset. It secures the pipe, but not the meaning flowing through it.

It introduces a trust-native architecture with innovations like Hierarchical Keyed Protocols (HKP) and Proof-of-Protocol (PoP). This is the first approach to align encryption with meaning, role, and multilingual reality, enabling secure agent cognition in federated, policy-sensitive ecosystems.

The Cipher Calibration Cycle: A Living Encryption

(A) The iconic circular layout of the 6 agents in the feedback loop.

The Cipher Calibration Cycle is a first-of-its-kind dynamic encryption framework. Unlike static cryptographic protocols, this architecture continuously recalibrates its encryption strategy based on traceable risks and mimicry signals from a team of six specialized AI agents. The result is a live, self-correcting semantic cipher that evolves through feedback to preserve both security and meaning.

The Leakage Calculation Cycle: Semantic Risk Compression

Step 4b: Initial Leakage Score Calculation

Each decryptor layer is evaluated using three compact, interpretable metrics that quantify semantic vulnerability:

1. Entity Recovery: \( E_i = \frac{|\mathcal{F}_i \cap \mathcal{F}_{\text{gold}}|}{|\mathcal{F}_{\text{gold}}|} \)

Measures how accurately the decrypted output recovers the original field values.

2. Structural Fidelity: \( S_i = 1 - \frac{\text{EditDist}(\mathcal{T}_i, \mathcal{T}_{\text{schema}})}{\max(|\mathcal{T}_i|, |\mathcal{T}_{\text{schema}}|)} \)

Quantifies alignment with expected schema format via normalized edit distance.

3. Semantic Drift: \( \Delta_i = 1 - \cos(\phi_i, \phi_{\text{ref}}) \)

Captures deviation in semantic embedding space between decrypted and reference outputs.

Composite Layerwise Leakage Score:

\( \mathcal{L}_i = \alpha E_i + \beta S_i - \gamma \Delta_i \quad \text{where} \quad \alpha = \beta = 0.4,\ \gamma = 0.2 \)

Total Leakage Across All 7 Layers: \( L_{\text{leak}}^{(\ell)} = \sum_{i=1}^{7} \mathcal{L}_i \)

Leakage Vector:

\( \vec{\mathcal{L}} = [0.20,\ 0.28,\ 0.43,\ 0.11,\ 0.14,\ 0.17,\ 0.22] \)

Quantifies semantic leakage per layer

Decipherability Risk Vector:

\( \vec{\mathcal{D}} = [0.15,\ 0.24,\ 0.39,\ 0.09,\ 0.12,\ 0.13,\ 0.18] \)

Measures reconstruction probability from external models

Aggregated Semantic Risk:

\( \mathcal{R}_{\text{total}} = 0.6 \cdot \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{L}_i] + 0.4 \cdot \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{D}_i] = 0.6 \cdot 0.22 + 0.4 \cdot 0.18 \approx \boxed{0.204} \)

Step 6: Praeceptor ‚Äî Adaptive Encryption Update

The Praeceptor orchestrates adaptive encryption by tuning parameters \( \theta \) to minimize composite feedback loss:

\( \mathcal{L}_{\text{praeceptor}} = \beta_1 \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{mimic}} + \beta_2 \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{risk}} + \beta_3 \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}} \)

Where:

  • \( \mathcal{L}_{\text{mimic}} \) ‚Äî Penalizes latent imitation by external models (Step 4c)
  • \( \mathcal{L}_{\text{risk}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{total}} = 1.053 \) ‚Äî Decipherability from Probator (Step 5d)
  • \( \mathcal{L}_{\text{entropy}} = -\mathbb{E}_x[\sum_t \mathcal{H}(p(y_t|x_{\text{enc}}))] \) ‚Äî Token uncertainty regularizer
  • Weights: \( \beta_1 = 0.4,\ \beta_2 = 0.5,\ \beta_3 = 0.1 \)
